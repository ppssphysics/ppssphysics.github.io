Introduction

This website consists in a visualisation product that was built using Shiny in RStudio in the context of the final project assignment for the last module of the Data Science Coursera specialisation>: Developing Data Products.

The product features a very brief analysis of a publically available data set presenting the results of a global survey conducted in 2016 by the Global Shapers Community, with the objective of strengthening the voice of youth across the world. This Community is a self-managed network of decentralized Hubs across more than 130 countries. The community was born 5 years ago as an initiative of the World Economic Forum and is powered today by more than 6000 young people from ages 20 to 29.

Through this dasboard, you can explore the results of the survey. More than 26000 young people aged from 18 to 35 answered the survey which was articulated around two leading questions: What do young people think about the world anbd what do they want to do about it? You can find out more about the survey and download the raw data here (shaperssurvey.org)

You can use the top tabs to naviguate through different contents related to the survey:

  • Introduction Tab:
    Your are currently reading the introduction to this product.
  • Participation Tab:
    In this tab, you will find statistics related to the survey participation. You can filter the overall participation numbers by the United Region categorsation but also by the World Economic Forum sub-region classification using the slide bar on the left. For each chosen filter, you will see a number of participation metrics, like the gender distribution or the status of the survey (completed or partial) evolve.
  • Insights Tab:
    You will find the results of two survey questions (among the many that were asked) that you can again explore using different filters in the slide bar.

This interactive dasboard is built using the shiny and shinydashboard librairies in R.
